01 — The Premise
Screens are never still.

Frank Chimero's What Screens Want opens with a provocation: the screen is not a canvas. It is a living surface, constantly in motion — even in silence.

02 — The Concept
He calls it flux.

Flux is the quality of ceaseless, quiet change. Not dramatic transformation — but the slow, ongoing shift that happens between one moment and the next. Time passing through a screen.

Right now, the screen is changing

You experience a screen across time — not all at once.

--:--:--

This moment has already passed. The screen continues.

03 — The Experience

The screen moves whether you watch or not. It is always in the process of becoming something else.

Chimero's insight is that designing for screens means designing for time — accepting that what you make will be seen differently in every moment it is viewed.

Screens have flux. Digital things always have the quality of change. This is what makes them special.

Slow Change

Flux isn't sudden. It's the imperceptible drift — pixels shifting, states updating, time becoming visible through the surface of glass.

02
Time as Medium

Unlike paint on canvas, screens exist in the fourth dimension. Duration is not a side effect — it is the material itself.

Experience Over Glance
